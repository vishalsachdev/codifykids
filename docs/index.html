Our Current Camp

Learn the basics of Artificial Intelligence while building a smart car with Littlebits, listening to guest speakers, and doing fun activities!

For Middle School Age girls only. Home schooled kids welcome. No coding experience necessary. Free camp funded by a support from the Library and a grant from AI4ALL. Lunch will be provided.

Presented in partnership with Codify Kids, Champaign Public Library, and Illinois MakerLab. Only 16 campers accepted.

Key People

Shreya Sachdev

codify guru

She is a fifteen-year-old sophomore. She participated in the AI4ALL summer camp set up by Stanford at Arizona State University, where students learned Python and the basics of Artificial Intelligence. She is also an ambassador for AI4ALL. She participates each year in the First Tech Competition and her robotics team, CTRL-Y, got into the semi-finals in 2019 and to State in 2020. Shreya is one of the main coders in the team. Throughout the season CTRL-Y helped out the younger teams of the First Lego League competition in coding, with their robots, and running the event. She has used JavaScript, basic HTML and CSS, Scratch, and is learning Python. She is a budding poet and is also SafeSitter certified. She has experience working with kids at Camp Invention. She has helped organize fundraising earlier for the soup kitchen by collecting 100 lb of food and 12 backpacks and volunteers regularly.

<plans>

Our Plans

We plan to raise $2000 dollars this year. We have raised $600 so far since summer 2018.

$600 raisedgoal $2000

We want to offer monthly weekend camps during Fall and Spring and create a regular club. Come summer, we plan to offer week-long camps again.

Our goal is to provide opportunities for every kid without asking for much. Every $100 we raise feeds another child for a year through Feeding Our Kids, our local charity — so kids can focus on their studies instead of looking for their next meal. Some of our camps are funded by grants.
